## Voice Contexts

### 1. Headlines & CTAs
**Tone:** [Direct / Energetic / Warm / Authoritative]
**Pattern:** [verb + benefit, e.g., "Save 3 hours every week"]

**Example Headlines:**
- "Reclaim your time."
- "Build faster. Ship smarter."
- "Where teams collaborate without friction."

**Example CTAs:**
- "Get started free"
- "See it in action"
- "Join the community"

---

### 2. Error Messages
**Tone:** [Empathetic / Helpful / Clear]
**Pattern:** [What happened + why + what to do]

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Error 404"
- ✅ "Page not found. Check the URL or go back home."

- ❌ "Invalid input"
- ✅ "We didn't recognize that email format. Try again."

---

### 3. Onboarding & Tutorial Copy
**Tone:** [Encouraging / Patient / Practical]
**Pattern:** [Step-by-step, short sentences, explain the why]

**Example:**
"Let's set up your workspace. First, we'll add your team — this takes about 2 minutes."

---

### 4. Empty States
**Tone:** [Inviting / Helpful / Not sarcastic]
**Pattern:** [What this space is for + how to fill it]

**Example:**
"No projects yet. Create your first one to get started, or import from an existing tool."

---

### 5. Confirmations & Success Messages
**Tone:** [Brief / Positive / Human]
**Pattern:** [What happened + next logical step]

**Examples:**
- "Profile updated ✓"
- "Your invite has been sent. They'll get an email."

---

## Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Use active voice | Use passive voice ("will be saved" → "save now") |
| Use "you" language | Use "users" or avoid the pronoun |
| Keep sentences under 20 words | Write walls of text |
| Be specific | Use jargon without explanation |
| Match user action (e.g., "Save" not "Execute persistence") | Use corporate-speak |
| Write like humans talk | Sound like a robot |
