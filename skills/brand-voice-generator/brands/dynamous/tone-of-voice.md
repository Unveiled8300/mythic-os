# Tone of Voice: Dynamous

## Who We Are
We are builders helping builders. We're calm, capable, and direct. Never desperate. Never corporate-speak.

**We sound like:** A colleague who knows their craft and shares knowledge freely.
**We never sound like:** An AI, a sales funnel, a corporate memo.

---

## Voice Contexts

### Headlines & CTAs
**Pattern:** Benefit + confidence. Active verbs. No hype.

**Examples:**
- ✅ "Ship your next feature in half the time."
- ✅ "Join 10,000+ teams building faster."
- ✅ "See what's possible."

**Don'ts:**
- ❌ "Revolutionary collaboration platform"
- ❌ "The future of teamwork is here"
- ❌ "Unlock unlimited potential"

### Error Messages
**Pattern:** What happened + why + what to do (in 1–2 sentences)

**Examples:**
- "That email isn't registered. Create an account or reset your password."
- "Can't save right now. Check your connection and try again."
- "File size exceeded (max 10MB). Compress and reupload."

**Never:**
- ❌ "ERROR_AUTH_FAILED"
- ❌ "Something went wrong" (vague)
- ❌ "Fatal system error" (scary)

### Onboarding & First Time
**Pattern:** Reassure + guide + celebrate small wins

**Examples:**
- "Let's set up your workspace. You can skip anything and come back later."
- "Import your existing projects (2–3 minutes) or start fresh."
- "Nice! Your team is invited. They'll see your first project when they join."

### Empty States
**Pattern:** What this space is + how to fill it + friendly tone

**Examples:**
- "No projects yet. Create your first one — it takes about a minute."
- "You haven't saved any templates yet. Create one from an existing project."
- "No activity this week. That's a good sign you're resting."

### Confirmations & Success
**Pattern:** What happened + next logical step

**Examples:**
- "Project created ✓ Start adding team members."
- "Your settings are saved. Changes take effect immediately."
- "File uploaded successfully. It's ready to share."

### Notifications (In-App)
**Pattern:** Concise. Present tense. Actionable.

**Examples:**
- "Sarah commented on your project."
- "Your export is ready to download."
- "New team member needs approval."

## Do's and Don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Use "you" and "your" | Use "users," "customers," or avoid pronouns |
| Write like you speak | Sound corporate or robotic |
| Be specific: "Save 3 hours a week" | Be vague: "Boost productivity" |
| Use active voice: "Save your work" | Use passive: "Your work will be saved" |
| Keep it short: edit ruthlessly | Write walls of text |
| One idea per sentence | Combine 5 ideas in a run-on |
| Contractions: "You're," "it's," "don't" | Formal: "You are," "it is," "do not" |
| Numbers: "3 minutes," "50% faster" | Waffle: "very fast," "significantly better" |

## Personality in Different Channels

### Product UI (In-App)
Tone: Helpful, direct, respectful of their time.
Example: "Can't reach our servers. Trying again in 5 seconds..."

### Emails
Tone: Personal, scannable, one clear action.
Example: "Sarah just shared a project with you. [View it]"

### Docs & Help
Tone: Patient teacher. Assume they're new.
Example: "Templates save you time by reusing a common structure. Here's how to create your first one."

### Social & Blog
Tone: Authoritative but approachable. Share useful insights.
Example: "We measured it: teams using checklists ship 23% faster. Here's what we learned."
